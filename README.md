# CS-250 Agile & Scrum Portfolio Artifact

This repository contains my Sprint Review and Retrospective deliverable completed for CS-250 at Southern New Hampshire University.

## Portfolio Reflection

### Interpreting User Needs and Using User Stories
When interpreting user needs, I focus on understanding what the user is trying to accomplish rather than jumping straight into technical solutions. In this course, creating user stories helped turn vague requirements into clear, actionable goals by framing them from the user’s perspective. Writing stories in the form of “As a user, I want…” made it easier to prioritize functionality based on value and helped ensure that development work stayed aligned with real user expectations. User stories also made it easier to break larger features into smaller, testable pieces that could be reviewed and adjusted through feedback.

### My Approach to Developing Programs and Using Agile Processes
I approach program development as an iterative process rather than a one-time build. Throughout this course, Agile practices such as sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives reinforced the importance of continuous improvement and adaptability. Instead of trying to perfect everything upfront, I learned the value of delivering working increments, gathering feedback, and refining solutions over time. Moving forward, I plan to continue using Agile practices like short development cycles, regular reflection through retrospectives, and backlog refinement to keep my work flexible and focused on delivering value.

### Being a Good Team Member in Software Development
Being a good team member in software development means more than just completing assigned tasks. It involves clear communication, accountability, and respect for different roles within the team. Through activities like sprint reviews and retrospectives, I learned how important transparency and collaboration are to a team’s success. A good team member listens to feedback, adapts when plans change, and supports the team’s shared goals rather than focusing only on individual contributions.
